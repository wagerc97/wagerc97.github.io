About me

Questions in natural sciences become increasingly complex and require powerful tools to find answers. I realized this during my bachelor's degree of Biology at the University of Vienna (2016 - 2020). So, I wanted to enhance my broad knowledge of natural sciences with computer science and mathematics.
Hence, I decided to study the master's degree of Computational Science at the University of Vienna (2020 - 2024). Besides learning methods for Data Analysis and Scientific Computing during this master's program, my main interests include Machine Learning, Simulations and Modeling.
Continuing my academic eduction I pursue a doctor's degree at University of Vienna.

My Research

My research interests center on the development and application of advanced optimization techniques, particularly in tackling complex, multi-objective problems associated with permanent magnet design. I am dedicated to study novel chemical compositions of permanent magnetic materials that reduce rare earth content contributing to building more sustainable green energy technologies.
I have explored the effectiveness of active learning schemes as a method for minimizing computationally expensive objective functions, comparing its performance against traditional optimization algorithms. My work has involved creating a modular Python framework to conduct comprehensive experiments.
